## Current Issue to Be Addressed

Despite strong national policies like the **Singapore Green Plan 2030**, individual and community sustainability actions remain **invisible, unverified, and disconnected** from institutional goals and national reporting.

### The Three Critical Gaps

**1. No Verifiable Individual Climate Data**

Current reality at NUS:
- Students take public transport, eat plant-based meals, sort waste, volunteer—but **none of this is measured or trusted**
- Sustainability Office reports aggregate campus emissions but has **no visibility into individual behavioral patterns**
- ESG reporting relies on estimates and averages, not actual verified actions
- **Result**: NUS cannot credibly claim individual-level impact or identify what interventions actually work

**2. Low Translation from Awareness to Daily Behavior Change**

Students understand sustainability intellectually but:
- Daily habits remain unchanged because **actions are invisible and unrewarded**
- No feedback loop showing personal impact
- Sustainability feels like sacrifice rather than achievement
- **Result**: High survey scores on "sustainability awareness" but minimal behavior change in practice

**3. Missing Trust Layer Between Individuals, Institutions, and Policy**

There is no privacy-focused system that connects:
- What individuals actually do daily →
- What institutions need for ESG reporting →
- What national climate goals require for verification →
- What future carbon markets demand for credibility

**Result**: Singapore's strongest climate asset—an engaged, educated population—**remains unmobilized and unmeasured**.

### Why This Matters for Singapore

Without solving this, Singapore faces:
- **Greenwashing risk**: Institutional sustainability claims that can't be verified at the individual level
- **Missed behavior change potential**: Millions of small daily actions that could add up to measurable national emissions reductions
- **Talent gap**: Graduates entering the green economy without verifiable sustainability credentials
- **Market disadvantage**: Future carbon markets and ESG standards will require individual-level verification—Singapore risks falling behind

## Proposed Solution: Cero ID

Cero ID is a **privacy-first, decentralized sustainability identity** that turns daily climate-friendly actions into trusted, verifiable records—owned by individuals, valuable to institutions, and scalable to national climate goals.

### How It Works

**For Students:**
1. **One-time opt-in**: Sign up with NUS SSO (30 seconds)
2. **Automatic verification**: Actions tracked passively through existing campus systems
   - Building entry/exit (via access card) = sustainable commute detection
   - Cafeteria purchases (via meal plan) = plant-based meal verification
   - Bike-sharing usage (via existing app) = active mobility credits
   - Waste sorting (via smart bin sensors) = recycling confirmation
   - Event attendance (via QR check-in) = volunteering verification
3. **Earn Cero Credits**: 1 credit = 1 kg CO₂e avoided
4. **Redeem rewards**: Campus services, vouchers, career profile enhancement
5. **Own your data**: Portable sustainability identity that follows you after graduation

**For NUS Administration:**
1. **Real-time dashboard**: See campus-wide sustainability patterns by hall, faculty, demographic
2. **Automated ESG reporting**: Generate verified individual-level data for institutional reports
3. **Behavior change insights**: Understand which interventions actually work (with academic rigor)
4. **Recruitment advantage**: Market NUS as the first university producing graduates with verified sustainability credentials
5. **Research output**: Anonymized dataset for publishable behavioral science research

### Core Technical Architecture

**Privacy-First Design:**
- No raw personal data stored on-chain or centrally
- Zero-knowledge proofs for action verification
- User consent controls what data is shared and with whom
- PDPA and GDPR compliant by design

**Verification Without Surveillance:**
We don't try to verify everything—we start with **high-trust, low-friction** data sources:

| Action Category | Verification Method | Data Source | Trust Level |
|----------------|-------------------|------------|------------|
| Sustainable Commute | Building entry timing + bike-sharing API | Existing access card system | High |
| Plant-Based Meals | Cafeteria purchase records | Meal plan database | High |
| Waste Sorting | Smart bin sensors at collection points | Campus waste management | Medium-High |
| Volunteering | QR code check-in at official events | Campus clubs/orgs system | High |
| Energy Conservation | Building occupancy patterns | Smart building sensors | Medium |

**Key Insight**: We use data institutions already collect and trust, rather than creating new surveillance infrastructure.

**Blockchain-Lite Approach:**
- Core verification happens off-chain for speed and cost-efficiency
- Only action summaries (not personal identifiers) recorded on-chain for auditability
- Users receive portable credential they control (like a digital passport)

### What Makes Cero ID Different

**It's not a carbon calculator** (those just estimate)  
**It's not a gamification app** (those lack verification and portability)  
**It's not a corporate ESG platform** (those don't reach individuals)

**Cero ID is infrastructure**: A trust layer that makes individual climate action **measurable, ownable, and valuable** across institutions and over time.

## Competitive Landscape & Market Positioning

The sustainability behavior change market is fragmented between **engagement platforms** (high user numbers, low verification) and **ESG reporting tools** (high institutional value, no individual visibility). Cero ID is the first to bridge both.

### Existing Solutions & Their Limitations

**1. Gamification & Engagement Platforms**

*Ant Forest (Alipay/Alibaba)*
- **Scale**: 800M+ users in China
- **Strengths**: Proven massive user engagement through gamification; tangible tree-planting rewards; social features drive virality
- **Limitations**: 
  - Platform-controlled (Alibaba owns all data and user relationships)
  - Verification opaque (users trust Alipay, but actions aren't independently verified)
  - Not portable (impact tied to Alipay ecosystem)
  - No institutional value (companies can't use this for ESG reporting)
- **Lesson for Cero ID**: Gamification and tangible rewards work at scale; social proof drives engagement

*Oroeco, Joro, Earth Hero*
- **Scale**: 100K-1M users (primarily Western markets)
- **Strengths**: Better carbon calculation methodologies; focus on individual empowerment
- **Limitations**:
  - Self-reported data (low verification, high friction)
  - No institutional integration
  - Monetization struggles (hard to convert free users to paid)
- **Lesson for Cero ID**: Self-reporting doesn't scale; need passive verification

**2. Digital Identity Platforms**

*World ID (WorldCoin / Tools for Humanity)*
- **Scale**: 15M+ users globally
- **Strengths**: Portable global identity; biometric verification; cryptocurrency integration
- **Limitations**:
  - Not purpose-built for sustainability (general identity, no climate focus)
  - Regulatory challenges (biometric data collection banned in some countries)
  - Public skepticism around privacy and data usage
  - No verification of real-world climate actions
- **Lesson for Cero ID**: Portable identity is valuable, but biometrics are controversial; focus on behavior verification, not identity verification

*National Digital Identity Systems (SingPass, Aadhaar, etc.)*
- **Scale**: 100M+ users (India), 4M+ (Singapore)
- **Strengths**: Government backing; universal adoption; trusted infrastructure
- **Limitations**:
  - Government-controlled (not user-owned)
  - Not designed for sustainability actions
  - Privacy concerns around centralized data
- **Lesson for Cero ID**: Institutional trust matters; privacy-first design is differentiator

**3. Enterprise ESG & Carbon Management Platforms**

*Watershed, Persefoni, Sweep, Plan A*
- **Scale**: 1,000-10,000 enterprise customers globally
- **Strengths**: Robust Scope 1-3 emissions accounting; compliance-ready reporting; corporate sustainability team workflows
- **Limitations**:
  - Cannot measure individual employee behavior (the emerging "Scope 4")
  - Rely on industry averages and estimates for behavioral emissions
  - No employee engagement features
  - Enterprise-only (too expensive and complex for universities or cities)
- **Lesson for Cero ID**: Enterprise ESG software market is massive but has a blind spot—individual behavior. We fill that gap.

**4. University Sustainability Programs (Status Quo)**

*Current Tools at NUS and similar universities:*
- Periodic surveys and focus groups
- Aggregate campus utility data (electricity, water, waste)
- Participation counts for volunteer events
- Self-reported sustainability pledges

*Limitations:*
- No visibility into individual daily behavior
- No verification of claimed actions
- Difficult to measure behavior change over time
- Cannot produce audit-grade data for ESG reporting
- Intervention design is guesswork (no data on what works)

**Lesson for Cero ID**: Universities desperately need better tools but are underserved by existing sustainability tech (too focused on corporate market).
