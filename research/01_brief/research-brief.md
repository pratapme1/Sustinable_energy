# Research Brief
**Project**: Waste-to-Energy India — Wet Waste & Agricultural Waste
**Date**: 2026-04-02
**Status**: Active

---

## Decision To Support
Should Vi build a waste-to-energy business in India that converts wet waste and agricultural waste
into usable energy (biogas, biofuel, electricity, biomethane)?
If yes: which technology, which model, which segment first, and what does the path to scale look like?

## Objective
Determine the full viability — market, technical, economic, regulatory, and operational — of a
waste-to-energy business in India focused on wet waste and agricultural waste as feedstock,
with enough depth to pitch credibly to investors, align with government, and act as a founder's strategic playbook.

## Audience
| Audience | Primary Need |
|---|---|
| Investors | Opportunity size, defensibility, returns |
| Founder (Vi) | Strategic clarity and execution roadmap |
| Government | Public benefit, policy alignment |
| Citizens | Problem-solution narrative |

## Scope — What Is Included

### Problem Space
- Scale of wet waste and agricultural waste in India (volume, geography, seasonality)
- Current waste handling: what happens today, why it fails
- Environmental, health, and economic cost of current handling
- Global comparison

### Market
- India waste-to-energy market size (current + projected)
- Global waste-to-energy market for context
- Investment trends, funding flows
- Government schemes, subsidies, incentive programs
- Demand for energy from waste (CBG, biofuel, electricity)

### Technology
- Full map of technologies: Anaerobic Digestion, Pyrolysis, Gasification, Fermentation, Co-digestion
- Energy yield per ton for each waste stream
- Technology readiness, CAPEX, OPEX, efficiency
- Which technology fits which waste stream
- Global technology leaders and what can be imported/adapted

### Players & Competition
- India: existing waste-to-energy companies, startups, government plants
- Global: leading operators, technology providers, business models
- White spaces and competitive gaps

### Business Case
- Revenue models: energy sale, tipping fees, carbon credits, biofertilizer
- Unit economics: cost per ton processed, revenue per ton, margin
- CAPEX/OPEX by plant size
- Break-even analysis
- Funding sources: equity, debt, grants, subsidies

### Regulatory & Policy
- India: National Bioenergy Programme, MNRE schemes, CBG/SATAT scheme
- State-level policies
- Carbon credit eligibility
- Permits, clearances, compliance requirements
- Risk: policy reversal, subsidy withdrawal

### Implementation
- Waste collection and supply chain model
- Plant setup: land, equipment, utilities
- Operational model: who runs it, what skills needed
- Scale strategy: pilot → regional → national
- Partnership model

### Risk Analysis
- Technology risk
- Feedstock supply risk
- Regulatory risk
- Market/offtake risk
- Execution risk

### Stakeholder Adoption
- Farmer engagement for agri waste
- Municipality partnerships for wet waste
- Off-taker agreements (energy buyers)
- Community acceptance

## Out Of Scope
- Water and food waste beyond what overlaps with wet/agri waste streams
- Air quality as a primary focus (covered only as an impact metric)
- Non-organic waste streams (plastics, metals, e-waste)
- Export markets (India-first)

## Constraints
- Geography: India primary, global for technology and benchmarks
- Data: Public sources only; paywalled data triangulated from multiple public sources
- No internal operational data (greenfield project)

## Success Criteria
This research succeeds if:
1. A credible investor can read this and understand the TAM, model, and investment thesis
2. Vi can use this as a founder's playbook for Year 1 decisions
3. A government official can verify public benefit claims independently
4. Every major claim has at least one cited source
5. A clear recommendation exists on: which technology, which entry point, which model
