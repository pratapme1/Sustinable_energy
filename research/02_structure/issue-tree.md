# Issue Tree
**Project**: Waste-to-Energy India
**Date**: 2026-04-02

---

## Central Question
**Is building a waste-to-energy business in India (wet waste + agri waste → energy) viable, and what is the optimal path?**

---

## Branch 1: How Big Is the Problem?
> What is the actual scale of wet waste and agricultural waste in India?

- 1.1 How much wet waste does India generate annually? By city, by tier?
- 1.2 How much agricultural waste is generated? By crop, by state?
- 1.3 What is currently done with it? What percentage is processed vs dumped?
- 1.4 What is the economic, environmental, and health cost of the current approach?
- 1.5 Is the waste supply consistent, reliable, and accessible?

## Branch 2: What Is the Market Opportunity?
> How large is the commercial opportunity?

- 2.1 What is the India waste-to-energy market size today?
- 2.2 What is the projected growth rate and why?
- 2.3 What energy demand exists for outputs (CBG, biofuel, electricity)?
- 2.4 What government incentives, subsidies, and schemes exist?
- 2.5 Who is paying for energy from waste today — at what price?

## Branch 3: What Technology Works Best?
> Which conversion technology is the right fit?

- 3.1 What are the main technology pathways (AD, pyrolysis, gasification, fermentation)?
- 3.2 What energy yield does each produce per ton of each waste type?
- 3.3 What are the CAPEX, OPEX, and maintenance requirements for each?
- 3.4 What is the technology readiness level and track record at scale?
- 3.5 Which technology works for wet waste vs agri waste vs both?
- 3.6 Where does the best technology come from globally, and can it be deployed in India?

## Branch 4: Who Is Already Doing This?
> What is the competitive and ecosystem landscape?

- 4.1 Who are the active players in India (startups, corporations, govt plants)?
- 4.2 Who are the global leaders and what models do they use?
- 4.3 What business models are proven vs failing?
- 4.4 Where are the gaps — geographic, technological, segment?
- 4.5 What is the competitive moat available to a new entrant?

## Branch 5: Does the Business Case Work?
> Is this financially viable?

- 5.1 What are the revenue streams (energy sale, tipping fees, carbon credits, biofertilizer)?
- 5.2 What does it cost to build a plant (CAPEX by size: small/medium/large)?
- 5.3 What are operating costs per ton of waste processed?
- 5.4 What is the revenue per ton, and at what volume does this break even?
- 5.5 What funding is available (equity, debt, grants, subsidies)?
- 5.6 What returns can investors expect and over what timeline?

## Branch 6: What Does Policy and Regulation Look Like?
> Is the regulatory environment enabling or blocking?

- 6.1 What national schemes support waste-to-energy in India (SATAT, National Bioenergy Programme)?
- 6.2 What are the state-level variations in policy and support?
- 6.3 What permits, clearances, and compliance requirements apply?
- 6.4 What are the carbon credit and emissions benefit mechanisms?
- 6.5 What is the risk of policy reversal or subsidy withdrawal?

## Branch 7: Can It Be Built and Operated?
> What does implementation actually look like?

- 7.1 How do you collect and aggregate wet waste at scale?
- 7.2 How do you source agri waste reliably (farmer partnerships, aggregators)?
- 7.3 What infrastructure is needed (land, plant, utilities, grid connection)?
- 7.4 What operational skills and workforce are required?
- 7.5 What is the pilot-to-scale roadmap?
- 7.6 What partnerships are essential (municipalities, farmers, off-takers)?

## Branch 8: What Are the Risks?
> What could go wrong and how badly?

- 8.1 What are the technology failure risks?
- 8.2 What happens if feedstock supply is disrupted?
- 8.3 What if policy changes or subsidies are cut?
- 8.4 What if the off-taker (energy buyer) doesn't materialise?
- 8.5 What operational and execution risks exist?
- 8.6 What are the environmental and community risks?

---

## Priority Order for Research Execution
1. Branch 1 — Problem Size (foundation of everything)
2. Branch 3 — Technology (informs business model)
3. Branch 2 — Market Opportunity (informs investment thesis)
4. Branch 5 — Business Case (informs investor pitch)
5. Branch 4 — Competition (informs positioning)
6. Branch 6 — Policy (informs risk and opportunity)
7. Branch 7 — Implementation (informs roadmap)
8. Branch 8 — Risk (synthesized last)
