## Executive Summary

A 100 TPD mixed-feedstock CBG plant in Western UP / Haryana is a **bankable infrastructure project** with the following headline economics:

- **Total CAPEX**: ₹95–110 crore (net ₹85–100 crore after MNRE subsidy)
- **Annual gross revenue** (Year 3 steady state): ₹30–35 crore
- **EBITDA margin**: 72–78%
- **Project payback**: 3.5–4.5 years from commissioning
- **Project IRR**: 18–24%
- **Equity IRR**: 30–42% (leveraged; IREDA PSL debt at ~10%)
- **Minimum viable CBG output for bankability**: 15 TPD (industry consensus)

The economics are structurally strong because:
1. Feedstock cost is near-zero or negative (tipping fees from bulk generators offset or eliminate cost)
2. Offtake is guaranteed (SATAT 15-year OMC commercial agreement at ₹46/kg floor)
3. CAPEX is largely fixed infrastructure — OPEX as % of revenue is low once built
4. Government subsidies reduce equity requirement
5. IREDA PSL debt is accessible at concessional rates against OMC offtake as collateral

The primary financial risk is **Year 1–2 ramp-up** — plants typically run at 50–80% utilisation in the first two years while feedstock supply chains mature. This must be modelled honestly with investors.

## 1. CAPEX Structure

### 1.1 Reference Plant: 100 TPD Mixed Feedstock, 14 TPD CBG Output

Based on:
- HPCL Badaun benchmark: ₹133 crore for 100 TPD paddy straw (straw requires pretreatment → higher CAPEX than manure-dominant mix)
- Industry estimate: ₹25–80 crore for 100 TPD range (Standing Committee on Petroleum 2022-23)
- Scaled empirical data: 10 TPD CBG plant = ₹25 crore; scaling to 14 TPD ≈ ₹33–40 crore at small scale; commercial 100 TPD = ₹95–110 crore (economies of scale)
- Rule: biogas purification infrastructure = ~40% of total equipment CAPEX

**CAPEX Breakdown — 100 TPD Mixed Feedstock Plant (manure + press mud + food waste)**

| Component | Cost Range (₹ crore) | % of Total | Notes |
|-----------|---------------------|-----------|-------|
| Land (50 acres; state may provide free in UP) | 0–10 | 0–10% | State government incentive in UP may reduce to zero |
| Civil works (digesters, slabs, roads, boundary wall, buildings) | 22–28 | 23–26% | Largest single component |
| AD Equipment (CSTR digesters, agitators, slurry pumps, heat exchangers) | 18–22 | 19–21% | Key process equipment |
| Biogas upgrading system (PSA or membrane + H₂S removal) | 15–20 | 16–19% | ~40% of equipment cost |
| Compression & storage (CBG compressors, cascade storage, dispensing) | 8–12 | 8–11% | CBG compression to 200+ bar |
| Feedstock handling (trucks, shredder, reception pit, pre-mixer) | 5–8 | 5–7% | Logistics receiving infrastructure |
| Utility infrastructure (power connection, water, effluent treatment) | 4–7 | 4–6% | Grid connectivity is critical |
| Instrumentation & control (IoT sensors, SCADA, remote monitoring) | 2–3 | 2–3% | Competitive differentiator |
| Pre-operative (EIA, DPR, legal, licensing, testing) | 2–3 | 2–3% | 6–9 months pre-construction |
| Working capital (3 months OPEX buffer) | 8–12 | 8–11% | Required for commissioning phase |
| **Total CAPEX** | **₹95–110 crore** | **100%** | |

**Government support deductions**:
- MNRE Central Financial Assistance: up to ₹10 crore per project (max ₹4 crore per 4,800 kg/day capacity)
- State CAPEX support (UP): land (free/subsidised); possible additional state grants
- **Net CAPEX after subsidies: ₹85–100 crore**

**For investor modelling**: Use ₹100 crore as the base case net project cost. This is a conservative assumption — HPCL Badaun was ₹133 crore for straw-only (harder feedstock); a manure-dominant mixed-feedstock plant should be 15–25% cheaper.

## 2. OPEX Structure

### 2.1 Annual Operating Costs (100 TPD Plant, 14 TPD CBG Output)

| Cost Item | Annual Cost (₹ crore) | Notes |
|-----------|----------------------|-------|
| **Feedstock** | **₹0 to –₹2.5 crore** | Manure: free or ₹500/tonne. Press mud + food waste: tipping fees of ₹1,000–2,500/tonne = income, not cost. Net feedstock cost = zero or negative |
| Labour (35–45 staff: plant ops, drivers, security, admin, management) | ₹2.0–2.8 crore | ₹45,000/month average fully loaded; includes supervisory and management |
| Power (net purchase after biogas self-generation covers ~30–40% of plant needs) | ₹1.2–1.8 crore | At ₹8/unit commercial; ~150,000 units/month net |
| Maintenance & repairs (2–2.5% of equipment CAPEX/year) | ₹2.0–2.5 crore | Higher in year 1–2; stabilises after |
| Insurance (0.5% of total CAPEX/year) | ₹0.5–0.6 crore | Asset + liability + business interruption |
| Consumables (chemicals for H₂S removal, lab reagents) | ₹0.3–0.5 crore | |
| Transportation (digestate delivery to farms; feedstock pickup logistics) | ₹0.5–0.8 crore | Reduces as local relationships formalise |
| Administration, compliance, audit, certification | ₹0.5–0.75 crore | SWM, CPCB, VCS audit etc. |
| **Total OPEX (ex-feedstock)** | **₹7.0–9.75 crore/year** | |

**OPEX insight**: At ₹5–8 million per TPD of CBG output (industry benchmark), a 14 TPD plant = ₹7–11.2 crore/year OPEX. Our bottom-up estimate of ₹7.0–9.75 crore/year is consistent.

**Key differentiator**: Feedstock cost is the largest OPEX variable at most plants. The tipping-fee model (bulk generators paying to dispose) converts this from a cost to a revenue line — structural advantage vs straw-only plants (which must buy straw at ₹1,500–3,000/tonne).

### 3.2 Funding Structure

| Source | Amount (₹ crore) | % | Terms |
|--------|-----------------|---|-------|
| MNRE Central Financial Assistance | 10 | 10% | Grant — not repayable |
| IREDA / bank debt (PSL category) | 60 | 60% | 15-year tenor; ~10% interest; CBG SATAT offtake as collateral |

**Equity deployment**: ₹30 crore promoter equity for a plant generating ₹9.22 crore PAT in Year 3 = **30.7% ROE**. After debt is repaid (Year 8–10), free cash flow increases significantly.

### 3.3 Cash Flow Ramp-Up Model

Realistic utilisation by year of operation:

| Year | Utilisation | CBG Output (TPD) | Revenue (₹ crore) | EBITDA (₹ crore) | Free Cash Flow (₹ crore) |
|------|------------|-----------------|------------------|-----------------|------------------------|
| Year 1 | 50% | 7 | 16.7 | 9.5 | 3.5 (after debt service ₹10 crore) |
| Year 2 | 80% | 11.2 | 26.7 | 18.2 | 8.2 |
| Year 3 | 100% | 14 | 33.4 | 24.96 | 14.96 |
| Year 4+ | 100% | 14 | 34–36 | 25–27 | 15–17 |

*Debt service = interest + principal repayment (~₹10 crore/year in years 1–8 for ₹60 crore at 15-year amortisation)*

**Key risk zone**: Year 1. Revenue at 50% utilisation barely covers debt service. Mitigation: (a) anchor feedstock contracts signed before commissioning; (b) IREDA moratorium on principal (typically 12–18 months); (c) working capital buffer of ₹8–12 crore in CAPEX.

| Metric | Value | Basis |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Total CAPEX (net of subsidy) | ₹100 crore | HPCL Badaun benchmark; industry data |

## 4. Sensitivity Analysis

### 4.1 Single-Variable Sensitivity (Base Case: ₹33.4 crore revenue, ₹24.96 crore EBITDA)

| Variable | Base Case | Downside Scenario | EBITDA Impact | Project IRR Impact |
|----------|----------|------------------|--------------|-------------------|
| CBG price | ₹62/kg | ₹46/kg (SATAT floor) | –₹22.1 crore | ~12–14% IRR |
| Plant utilisation | 100% | 75% | –₹6.2 crore | ~15–18% IRR |
| Feedstock cost (if tipping fee fails) | ₹0 (tipping) | ₹1,500/tonne for all 100 TPD | –₹4.95 crore | ~16–20% IRR |
| CAPEX overrun | ₹100 crore | ₹130 crore (+30%) | No EBITDA impact; equity IRR drops | ~22–28% equity IRR |
| OPEX overrun | ₹8.4 crore | ₹12 crore (+43%) | –₹3.6 crore EBITDA | ~16–20% IRR |

**Combined downside (CBG @ ₹46/kg + 75% utilisation + no tipping fees)**:
- Revenue: ~₹16 crore; EBITDA: ~₹7–8 crore; DSCR: ~0.7–0.8× → **below debt service threshold**
- This is why ₹46/kg CBG floor + operational ramp-up + feedstock certainty must be addressed before financial close

**Key finding**: The economics are robust at ₹54/kg+ CBG and ≥85% utilisation. The floor risk is ₹46/kg + low utilisation simultaneously — this is only possible if the SATAT floor is reduced AND the plant underperforms. Both happening together is low probability but must be stress-tested with investors.

### 4.2 Revenue Mix Sensitivity

| Scenario | CBG Only | + Tipping Fees | + Carbon Credits | + Digestate (Phase 3) |
|----------|---------|---------------|-----------------|----------------------|
| Annual Revenue | ₹28.6 crore | ₹30.6 crore | ₹32.7 crore | ₹35.7 crore |
| EBITDA | ₹20.2 crore | ₹22.2 crore | ₹24.3 crore | ₹27.3 crore |
| Project IRR | ~16% | ~18% | ~20% | ~23% |

Demonstrates that diversifying revenue streams meaningfully improves IRR — each additional stream adds ~2% project IRR.

## 5. 5-Plant Portfolio Economics (Year 5)

### 5.1 Scale Case: 5 Plants × 100 TPD

| Metric | Single Plant | 5-Plant Portfolio |
|--------|------------|-----------------|
| Total CAPEX invested | ₹100 crore | ₹450–500 crore (economies of scale in EPC) |
| Total equity required | ₹30 crore | ₹120–130 crore |

| EBITDA | ₹25 crore | ₹115–130 crore |
| Combined PAT | ₹9 crore | ₹40–50 crore |
| CBG production | 14 TPD | 65–70 TPD = ~23,000 MT/year |

### 5.2 Institutional Investment Narrative

For a growth equity investor or PE fund:
- ₹120–130 crore equity investment
- 5-plant portfolio with SATAT OMC offtake contracts
- EBITDA ₹115–130 crore by Year 5
- At 8× EBITDA exit multiple (conservative for infrastructure): **₹920–1,040 crore enterprise value**
- Net equity value (after ₹300 crore debt): **₹620–740 crore**
- Equity MOIC (on ₹125 crore): **5–6×** over 5–7 years = **~35–45% IRR**

This is an institutional-grade return from an infrastructure-grade asset with government-backed offtake.
