## Executive Summary

After exhaustive research across 12 workstreams — problem space, waste stream segmentation, technology pathways, India market baseline, global benchmarks, customer and revenue model, competitor landscape, unit economics, operating model, policy environment, and risk analysis — the entry strategy is clear and supported by convergent evidence.

**The Thesis in One Sentence**:
Build modular 100 TPD co-digestion CBG plants in the Western UP–Haryana agri-industrial corridor, anchored by formal feedstock contracts with dairy cooperatives and sugar mills, selling CBG to OMCs under SATAT offtake agreements, with digestate monetised as certified biofertiliser — a model no current competitor has replicated at this geography and scale.

**The Core Insight**:
India generates organic waste permanently and unavoidably. The question is whether it decomposes in landfills (releasing methane), burns in fields (releasing particulates and CO₂), or feeds a biogas plant (producing clean fuel and biofertiliser). The venture's job is to make the third option the most economically rational choice for farmers, bulk generators, and sugar mills — then collect the CBG revenue.

## 1. Beachhead: Western UP — Muzaffarnagar/Meerut Triangle

### 1.1 Why Here, Not Somewhere Else

| Decision Criterion | Western UP | Why Not Elsewhere |
|-------------------|-----------|------------------|
| Feedstock density | Highest multi-feedstock density in India: dairy belt + sugar belt + agri belt | Gujarat: competitively crowded (AMUL, 20 plants operational). Punjab: straw-only, seasonal, logistics-complex |
| CBG ecosystem maturity | 32 operational plants (most in India); vendors, contractors, EPC track record | Maharashtra: large MSW but contamination risk, weak policy |
| State government support | Free land for CBG plants; UPNEDA facilitation; CM-level commitment | Andhra Pradesh: Reliance already committing 500 plants |
| Real-world benchmark | HPCL Badaun (₹133 crore/100 TPD) is 90 minutes away — same supply chain | No other state has this benchmark proximity |
| Competition | EverEnviro is in Sangrur (Punjab) + eastern UP (Balarampur, Lakhimpur). Western districts (Muzaffarnagar, Meerut, Saharanpur) are largely unclaimed | EverEnviro gap in western districts is the entry window |
| SWM 2026 enforcement | Large hotels, hospitals, malls in Meerut, Aligarh, Agra — immediate tipping fee customers | UP is the most densely populated state; highest bulk generator count |

**First plant location**: Muzaffarnagar district or Meerut district, Uttar Pradesh.
- Primary feedstock: Muzaffarnagar is India's largest sugarcane processing district — unlimited press mud supply from Triveni, Balrampur, DSCL mills within 30 km radius
- Secondary feedstock: Dairy farms in the Meerut–Muzaffarnagar–Saharanpur triangle; UP produces 3 MMT cow dung/day
- Tipping fee feedstock: Institutional food waste from Meerut (pop 3.4 million) and Muzaffarnagar (pop 4.1 million)

### 1.2 First Plant Specification

| Parameter | Specification | Basis |
|-----------|--------------|-------|
| Total feedstock input | 100 TPD | HPCL Badaun benchmark; SATAT LoI minimum |
| Feedstock mix | 50 TPD cattle manure + 30 TPD press mud (Oct–Apr) or food waste + 20 TPD food waste/straw | Year-round supply; tipping fee on 50 TPD |
| CBG output | 14 TPD (14,000 kg/day) | 50–55 kg CBG per tonne feedstock; HPCL Badaun confirmed |
| Plant design | CSTR anaerobic digester + PSA upgrading (Phase 1) + IoT monitoring | Proven tech stack; serviceable domestically |
| Location | Muzaffarnagar district; 40–50 acres; preferably state-owned land | UP CM land offer; adjacency to sugar mills |
| Total CAPEX | ₹100 crore (net of MNRE subsidy) | Benchmarked; ₹7.1 crore/TPD CBG |
| Funding structure | ₹10 crore MNRE grant + ₹60 crore IREDA debt + ₹30 crore equity | Standard SATAT project finance structure |
| Timeline to first CBG | 18 months from financial close | Regulatory + construction + commissioning |

**Plant 3 location**: Saharanpur or Bijnor district, UP (adjacent to Plant 1 geography)

**Capital deployed**: ₹60 crore equity (institutional growth equity raise, Series A)
**Revenue by Month 42**: ₹50–70 crore annualised (3 plants at 80–100% utilisation)

### Phase 3: Expand (Months 36–60) — Plants 4 & 5 + Platform Development

**Goal**: 5 plants operational; become the leading independent CBG operator in North India; position for exit or Series B.

**Plant 4**: Gujarat dairy cooperative belt (Banaskantha or Sabarkantha)
- B2B partnership with smaller dairy cooperative (not AMUL/Banas — too competitive)
- Sugar mill press mud + cooperative dung + CGD injection
- Different geography builds portfolio diversification

**Plant 5**: Maharashtra sugar belt (Kolhapur or Sangli district)
- Sugar mill press mud + spent wash + press mud tipping fee model
- Maharashtra's 22,632 TPD MSW is Phase 5 or 6 — not Phase 3

**Platform development (Phase 3)**:
- FCO-certified biofertiliser product launched ("BioXcel" or similar brand)
- VCS carbon credits first issuance (Year 3 from commissioning = ~Month 30 for Plant 1)
- Membrane upgrading replaces PSA on Plant 1 as capacity expands and grid injection approached
- Central SCADA/IoT platform monitoring all 5 plants remotely
- Second raise: Series B or infrastructure fund acquisition of portfolio

**Capital deployed**: ₹90 crore equity (Series B or infrastructure fund)
**Revenue by Month 60**: ₹150–170 crore annualised (5 plants)
**EBITDA by Month 60**: ₹110–130 crore
**Enterprise value at exit (8× EBITDA)**: ₹880–1,040 crore

## 4. The Competitive Moat — Why This Is Defensible

| Moat Layer | Description | Time to Build | Time for Competitor to Copy |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|---------------------------|
| **Feedstock supply contracts** | Signed 3–5 year agreements with dairy cooperatives, sugar mills, bulk generators | 6–9 months | 2–3 years (same farms; exclusivity clauses) |
| **SATAT Commercial Agreements** | 15-year OMC offtake agreements; limit per geography how many more can get OMC priority | 18 months | Competitor needs their own plant; same timeline |
| **Operational track record** | 3 commissioned, running plants with DSCR >2× — bankable proof of concept | 36 months | 36 months minimum |
| **Trained workforce** | Plant operators, process engineers, Feedstock Logistics Managers trained on this specific multi-feedstock model | 24 months | Same; and they would recruit from our trained pool |
| **Digestate brand** | FCO-certified biofertiliser with farmer distribution network | 36–48 months | Same certification; but network takes longer |
| **IREDA relationship** | Known developer to IREDA; financing faster, better terms for subsequent plants | 24 months | Same process for any new developer |

**The moat is the combination**: Any single element can be copied. All six together, in this geography, take 3–5 years to replicate from scratch — which is precisely when Reliance's national rollout might reach North India. By then, the venture is an acquisition target, not a competitive threat to absorb.

## 7. The Pitch Narrative — One Page

**Problem**: India generates 160,000 TPD of urban wet waste and 500–650 MT/year of agricultural residue. 85–90% of crop residue is burned (44,000–98,000 deaths/year). Organic waste in landfills generates methane — 15% of India's total GHG emissions. There is no dominant private solution.

**Opportunity**: India has a sustainable CBG production potential of 90 bcm/year — 120% of national gas demand. Only 108 of the 1,094 SATAT-registered plants have been commissioned (10% rate). The execution gap exists because of operational failure, not policy failure. The market is waiting for a professional operator who solves feedstock aggregation, digestate monetisation, and plant operations simultaneously.

**Solution**: Modular 100 TPD co-digestion CBG plants in the Western UP–Haryana agri-industrial corridor. Multi-feedstock (cattle manure + press mud + institutional food waste) eliminates seasonal supply risk. Tipping-fee model from SWM 2026-mandated bulk generators makes feedstock free or revenue-generating. CSTR + PSA technology from Praj (140+ plants in India). Four revenue streams: CBG (₹62–72/kg, 15-year SATAT OMC agreement) + tipping fees + carbon credits + digestate biofertiliser.

**Why now**: CBO mandate is mandatory from April 2026 (1% → 5% by FY2029). SWM Rules 2026 came into force April 1, 2026, creating the tipping-fee customer base. Reliance is in Andhra Pradesh — the North India corridor is open for 3–5 years. Western UP has free state land, UPNEDA facilitation, and 32 operational CBG plants proving the ecosystem.

**Ask**: ₹30 crore Series A equity for Plant 1 (Muzaffarnagar, UP). First CBG sold in 18 months. Full utilisation by Month 24. ₹30–35 crore revenue, ₹22–25 crore EBITDA by Year 3. 5-plant platform by Year 5; ₹150–170 crore revenue; ₹110–130 crore EBITDA. Exit at 8× EBITDA = ₹1,040 crore EV. Equity MOIC: ~4× in 5–6 years.

**The team builds the moat**: Feedstock contracts, OMC offtake agreements, operational track record, and trained workforce — assembled in 36 months before any conglomerate's national rollout reaches this geography.
