- **Field burning**: Chittoor farmers burn groundnut shells and paddy straw seasonally
- **Lost energy**: 80–100 TPD of high-calorific organic material decomposes in landfills, releasing methane to atmosphere with zero economic return

## SLIDE 5 — FEEDSTOCK DEEP DIVE

### Stream 1: City Organic Waste (40 TPD) — Tipping Fee Model

Tirupati Municipal Corporation (TMC) already collects 130 TPD of organic/wet waste. Only 50 TPD is processed by the existing bio-methanation unit (raw biogas, not CBG). The remaining 80 TPD has no processor.

**Our arrangement**: TMC pays us a tipping fee to receive and process their residual organic fraction (40 TPD initially).
- Benchmark tipping fee: ₹800–1,500/tonne (Swachh Bharat Mission data; Indore ₹1,080/t)
- Revenue from TMC: ₹38–72 lakh/year at 40 TPD

**SWM 2026 trigger**: From 1 April 2026, bulk generators (hotels, institutions >100 kg/day) must process waste or pay Extended Producer Responsibility charges. This creates a second, parallel tipping fee customer base in Tirupati's hospitality sector.

### Stream 2: Institutional Food Waste — Pilgrim Economy (20 TPD)

Tirupati hosts hundreds of hotels, dharamshalas, and restaurants serving pilgrims. Conservative estimate: 50 major establishments each generating 200–400 kg/day food waste = **10,000–20,000 kg/day = 10–20 TPD**.

**TTD city-level establishments**: TTD operates guest houses, rest houses, and administrative canteens in Tirupati city (not just Tirumala). These are separate from the Tirumala hilltop IOCL plant and represent a natural partnership opportunity for tipping fee or waste supply agreement.

**Revenue**: Institutional food waste commands ₹1,000–2,000/tonne tipping fee (Delhi benchmark ₹2,000–2,700/t; Indore ₹1,080/t). At ₹1,000/t: 20 TPD × ₹1,000 × 330 days = **₹66 lakh/year**.

### Stream 3: Dairy Cattle Manure — Chittoor Belt (25 TPD)

Chittoor district (which includes Tirupati) is Andhra Pradesh's leading dairy district:
- 18–20 lakh litres daily milk collection
- ~80% farmers depend on dairy
- 2,173 Gokulam cattle sheds sanctioned under state scheme

Each litre of milk → approximately 3–4 kg wet manure. 18 lakh litres/day = **54,000–72,000 TPD manure** across the district. We need only 25 TPD — less than 0.05% of district production.

**Cost**: Manure is typically free or ₹0–200/tonne at source. Hub-and-spoke collection within 30 km radius.
**Co-digestion value**: Cattle manure stabilises digester pH, improves biogas yield 25–50% in co-digestion with food waste (literature verified).

### Stream 4: Groundnut Shells (5 TPD, Seasonal Enhancement)

Chittoor district grows significant groundnut. Groundnut shells are lignocellulosic:
- Need mild alkaline pretreatment (NaOH) before AD — standard practice
- Co-digestion with food waste at 50:50 ratio improves biomethane yield by 32% (Springer 2025 study)
- We use as seasonal yield booster, not primary feedstock
- Cost: near zero (waste material, farmer pays to dispose)

> *Sources: Swachh Bharat Mission Tirupati; SWM Rules 2026; Dairy Chronicle Chittoor 2024; Springer Nature 2025 (groundnut co-digestion); SBM Urban waste data*

## SLIDE 6 — TECHNOLOGY STACK

### Proven. Modular. Replicable.

We use no experimental technology. Every component has been commercially deployed in India.

| Component | Choice | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Digester type | CSTR (Continuous Stirred Tank Reactor) | Handles mixed feedstocks (food waste + manure + shells); dominant in India; Praj, Enviropol supply locally |
| Operating temperature | Mesophilic (35–38°C) | Lower energy input than thermophilic; stable with Tirupati's warm climate (avg 30°C+) |
| Biogas upgrading | PSA (Pressure Swing Adsorption) | Phase 1 technology; no water use; widely deployed in AP/South India; upgrades raw biogas to >95% CH₄ |
| CBG compression | CNG-standard compression train | Road tanker filling at 200–250 bar |
| Digestate handling | Solid-liquid separator → FCO-certified biofertiliser | Dry cake to groundnut/paddy farmers; liquid fraction for irrigation |
| Monitoring | IoT sensors (pH, temperature, gas yield) | Remote SCADA; VCS-compliant data archiving for carbon credits |

### Technology Reference: HPCL Badaun (UP) Benchmark

The HPCL-Praj plant at Badaun (commissioned January 2024) is our closest commercial comparator:
- Scale: 100 TPD feedstock (paddy straw)
- Output: 14 TPD CBG
- CAPEX: ₹133 crore gross
- Technology: RenGas™ by Praj Industries (CSTR + PSA)
- Status: Operational, supplying HPCL under SATAT

We adapt this proven configuration for Tirupati's multi-feedstock mix (food waste + manure + shells vs. straw). Multi-feedstock actually **reduces technology risk** — no single feedstock failure shuts the plant.

### Why Not Other Technologies?

| Alternative | Why We Reject for Phase 1 |
|---|---|
| Biomass gasification (thermal) | High moisture feedstock (food waste) incompatible; requires dry feedstock |
| Direct combustion / incineration | High CAPEX; regulatory complexity; public opposition in pilgrim city |
| Landfill gas capture | Tirupati landfill too small; gas quality variable |
| Composting only | No energy revenue; low tipping fee; not CBG |

3. **EPC with commissioning guarantee** — Praj RenGas™ or equivalent; performance bond tied to CBG quality specs
4. **Digestate before commissioning** — farmer agreements signed before first digestate flows; no disposal crisis
5. **APPCB early engagement** — CTE delays are the most common India CBG plant delay; start regulatory conversations in Month 0

### What Breaks the Thesis (Triple Downside)

The investment thesis breaks only if ALL of the following occur simultaneously:
1. CBG price falls to ₹40/kg (below SATAT floor — requires government policy reversal)
2. Utilisation stays below 55% for 24+ months (requires all 4 feedstock streams to fail simultaneously)
3. Digestate has zero market (requires AP groundnut/paddy farming to collapse)

**No historical precedent for this combination in India's CBG sector.**

## SLIDE 17 — THE TIRUPATI THESIS IN ONE PAGE

### Hypothesis
Tirupati generates 130 TPD of organic waste from a pilgrim economy that never stops. Converting this waste to Compressed Biogas creates a permanent resource from a permanent liability — and the government will pay for the output under a 15-year contract.

### Business Thesis
- **Feedstock**: Urban organic waste + institutional food waste (hotels/TTD city) + Chittoor dairy manure → 100 TPD consistent, year-round supply
- **Technology**: CSTR anaerobic digestion + PSA upgrading → CBG (proven at Badaun, Praj benchmark)
- **Revenue**: 4 streams (CBG + tipping fee + digestate + carbon) → ₹32 crore/year by Year 3
- **Policy**: SATAT 15-year OMC offtake + MNRE grant + AP ICEP 2024 incentives
- **Moat**: First SATAT plant in Tirupati + TTD city partnership + Chittoor dairy manure exclusivity + digestate farmer lock-in

### Why Now
1. **SWM Rules 2026 (1 April 2026)**: Hotels, dharamshalas, large kitchens must process or pay — our tipping fee offer is immediately relevant
2. **CBO mandate**: OMCs must buy CBG at 1% → 5% blend; Tirupati HPCL CNG demand is growing with pilgrimage traffic
3. **AP ICEP 2024**: State government is actively courting CBG investment; first entrants get maximum incentives
4. **Only 1 SATAT plant in AP**: Gap is wide open; Tirupati has the richest urban organic feedstock base in the state
